Teletskoye Lake, Altai

“Altyn-Kel” is the “golden lake”, as local residents call the largest lake in Altai and one of the largest lakes in Russia. The modern name, Teletskoe, has been known for just over 400 years and is associated with the Teles tribes that inhabited its shores at that time.

Multinskie Lakes, Altai

Multinskie lakes are a group of mountain lakes on the northern slope of the Katunsky ridge in the Altai Mountains. The lakes are located in Central Altai at elevations of 1710 and 1740 m. They are located on the territory of the Ust-Koksinsky district of the Altai Republic of Russia. They are located in the large river basin of the Multa River, 20 km from the village of Multa.

Ukok Plateau, Altai

Ukok is a plateau in the extreme south of the Altai Republic, at the junction of the state borders of Kazakhstan, China, Mongolia and Russia. In general, Ukok is a relic of a highly elevated hilly-western and ridge-western leveling surface with prevailing absolute heights of 2200-2500 m, above which mountain ranges rise on average by 500-600 m.

Katun River, Altai

The Katun originates on the southern slope of the Katunsky ridge at the foot of Mount Belukha, at the southern tip of the Gebler Glacier

Patmos island, Altai

Patmos is a small island, which is a rock with steep walls on the Katun River, Chemalsky district, Altai Republic. Located on the outskirts of the village of Chemal. Not far from the island is the confluence of the Katun and Chemal rivers. The only way to the island is through a suspension bridge.
